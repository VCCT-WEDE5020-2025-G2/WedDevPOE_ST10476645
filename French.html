Ratatouille

A traditional french dish of stewed vegetables, such as tomato, garlic, onions, courgette, aubergines, bell peppers and herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 large eggplants
  • 1 large Zucchini
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 yellow bell pepper
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 large tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Wash and cut the eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and onion into 1/2-inch cubes. Mince the garlic and chop the tomatoes if using fresh ones. (Note the vegetables need to be cut into slices)
  2. Cook the Eggplant: Heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add the eggplant cubes and cook for about 5-7 minutes until they start to soften. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Cook the Zucchini and Bell Peppers: In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of olive oil. Add the zucchini and bell peppers, cooking for another 5-7 minutes until they begin to soften. Remove and set aside with the eggplant.
  4. Sauté the Onions and Garlic: Add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil to the skillet. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  5. Add Tomatoes and Herbs: Stir in the chopped tomatoes (or canned tomatoes) into the skillet with the onions and garlic. Cook for about 5 minutes until the tomatoes start to break down. Add dried thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper to taste.
  6. Return the cooked eggplant, zucchini, and bell peppers to the skillet. Stir everything together to combine well. Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all vegetables are tender and flavors meld together.
  7. To serve: Garnish with fresh basil or parsley if desired. Ratatouille can be served hot or at room temperature as a main dish or side dish.

Croque Monsieur

A classic French grilled ham and cheese sandwich topped with creamy béchamel sauce and melted cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices of bread
  • 2 slices of ham
  • 2 slices of cheese
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons of flour
  • 1 cup of milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. For the béchamel:
  2. In a saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Gradually add the milk and whisk until the sauce slightly thickens. Add salt and pepper to taste (optionally you can add cheese to the béchamel).
  4. For the sandwich:
  5. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C).
  6. Spread a thin layer of béchamel sauce on one side of each slice of bread.
  7. Place a slice of ham and a slice of cheese on one slice of bread, then top with the other slice of bread, béchamel side down.
  8. Spread a thin layer of béchamel sauce on top of the sandwich and sprinkle with grated cheese.
  9. Place the sandwich on a baking sheet and bake for 10 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your Croque Monsieur!

French Onion Soup

A classic French soup made with caramelized onions, beef broth, and topped with melted cheese and toasted bread.

Ingredients:

  • 2 large onions
  • 2 cups beef stock
  • 1 cup white wine (red wine works too)
  • Olive oil
  • Butter
  • Salt, pepper and garlic to taste
  • Baguette slices
  • Grated swiss or Gruyere cheese
  • Thyme and bay leaves (optional)

Steps:

  1. Start by caramelising your onions.
  2. Once your onions have softened, season them to taste with salt and pepper and add a tablespoon of butter in the pan to enhance the flavour.
  3. Once the onions have browned, add your garlic and cook for some more.
  4. Now add your wine to the pan.
  5. Once your wine has been added, pour the beef stock and some bay leaves and thyme and let it simmer for 25 minutes.
  6. While your soup is simmering, toast your baguette slices with your grated cheese on top.
  7. To serve, plate your soup in a bowl and place the cheesy slices of bread on top. Now enjoy your French onion soup!
