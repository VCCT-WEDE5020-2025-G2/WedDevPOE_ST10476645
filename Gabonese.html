Atanga

The atanga a popular gabonese savory snack.

Ingredients:

  • Atangas
  • salt
  • chilli(optional if not to tate)
  • Water for boiling
  1. Wash your atangas
  2. Then boil some water as if making pasta
  3. When boiling enough set aside then put the atanga in the hot water
  4. While in the water the atanga should start to soften
  5. To serve, take the sotten atangas out of the water and season to tate.
  6. Enjoy your atangas!

Bouillon de poisson(Fish Soup)

Bouillon de poisson is a traditional Gabonese fish soup made with fresh fish, vegetables, and spices, often served with plaintain or manioc(cassava) and other types of cassava.

Ingredients:

  • Fresh fish (such as tilapia)
  • Onions, chopped
  • Tomatoes,chopped
  • Okra
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh herbs (such as bay leaves)

Steps:

  1. Clean and cut the fish into pieces. Season with salt and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add chopped onions, saut√© until fragrant.
  3. Add okra to the vegetables
  4. Add the cut pieces of fish and add around 2 cups of water
  5. Let it simmer until the fish flesh is tender and fragrant
  6. Serve hot with plaintain, manioc(cassava), or rice.
  7. Enjoy your bouillon!

Poulet Nyembwe (Chicken Nyembwe)

Chicken Nyembwe is a savory meal stew made by simmering chicken(often smoked) in a thick and creamy sauce derived from palm nuts. It is usally served with rice , cassava or plaintain

Ingredients:

  • Chicken (smoked is recommended)
  • Palm nut cream (400g to 500g)
  • 2 large Onions(optional if not taste)
  • Garlic
  • 2 Tomatoes(optional if not taste)
  • 2 Chilli peppers(optional if not taste)
  • Red palm oil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Water( 1 litre or 4 cups)

Steps:

    Prep the chicken(note that this step is only under the circumstance that you are not using smoked chicken):

  1. If using unsmoked chicken, season it with salt, pepper, garlic and let it marinate for at least 30 min
  2. In a pot, heat the red oil palm oil (or any other oil if not using) and brown the chicken pieces on all sides. Removes the chicken and set aside.

    3. Make the palm nut sauce

  3. In the same pot heat the red oil palm oil (or any other oil if not using) saute the onions and garlic until soft and frangrant
  4. Add the tomatoes(if using) and cook for a few minutes until they break down slightly
  5. Stir in the palm nut cream and slowly add the water while stirring until the consistency gets thick and smooth

    6. Finishing the stew

  6. Season to taste and add the either smoked or browned chicken to the pot
  7. Add chillies(if using)
  8. Cover the pot and let gthe stew simmer gently for 45 minutes to an hour( make to stir and stop the fire when the consistency is to your liking)
  9. Enjoy your chicken Nyembwe with eith cassava, rice or plaintain!
