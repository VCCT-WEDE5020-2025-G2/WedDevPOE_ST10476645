Chinese Fried Rice

A classic Chinese dish made with stir-fried rice, vegetables, and your choice of protein, seasoned with soy sauce and sesame oil.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, corn)
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup diced cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add a bit of oil and scramble the beaten eggs until fully cooked. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  2. Add a bit more oil to the skillet and sauté the garlic and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Add the mixed vegetables and cook until tender.
  4. If using, add the diced cooked chicken, shrimp, or tofu to the skillet and stir-fry for a couple of minutes.
  5. Add the cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry for a few minutes until heated through.
  6. Add the soy sauce and sesame oil, stirring to combine everything evenly.
  7. Return the scrambled eggs to the skillet and mix them into the rice.
  8. Add the sliced green onions and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Chinese fried rice!

Chinese Braised Pork Belly (Hong Shao Rou)

A rich and flavorful dish featuring tender pork belly braised in a savory and slightly sweet soy-based sauce.

Steps:

  • 1 lb pork belly, cut into cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
  • 1 cup water or chicken broth
  • 3-4 slices ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 star anise
  • 2 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • Salt to taste

Steps:

  1. Blanch the pork belly cubes in boiling water for 2-3 minutes to remove impurities. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the sugar and cook until it melts and turns a caramel color.
  3. Add the blanched pork belly cubes to the pot and stir to coat them in the caramelized sugar.
  4. Add the soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine. Stir to combine.
  5. Add the water or chicken broth, ginger slices, smashed garlic, star anise, and green onions. Bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the pork belly is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  7. Season with salt to taste. Remove the ginger, garlic, star anise, and green onions before serving.
  8. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your delicious Chinese braised pork belly!

Chicken Chow Mein

A popular Chinese stir-fried noodle dish made with tender chicken, vegetables, and savory sauce

Ingredients

  • Chow mein noodles
  • Boneless chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • Mixed vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, bean sprouts)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Green onions for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the chow mein noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the sliced chicken and cook until fully cooked. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the remaining vegetable oil. Sauté the minced garlic until fragrant.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables and stir-fry until they are tender-crisp.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and mix with the vegetables.
  6. Add the cooked noodles to the skillet. Pour in the soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Toss everything together to combine well.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until everything is heated through.
  8. Garnish with sliced green onions if desired. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Chicken Chow Mein!

Chinese Crepes (Jian Bing/煎饼)

A popular Chinese street food, Jian Bing are savory crepes filled with egg, scallions, cilantro, and crispy wonton wrappers, often drizzled with hoisin and chili sauce.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup crispy wonton wrappers
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup water (adjust for a smooth batter)
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 chopped spring onions
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce or sweet bean paste
  • 2 tablespoons soybean paste
  • 1 tablespoons of chilli sauce(or more as it is optinal)
  • coriander (optional)
  • lettuce leaves(optional)

Steps

    For the batter:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour and water until smooth(like thin pancake batter)

    2. For the crepes:

  2. Heat a non-stick pan with a little oil
  3. Pour a ladle of batter, swirl to make a thin crepe
  4. Crack an egg over the crepe, spread it evenly
  5. Sprinkle chopped spring onions over the egg
  6. Once the egg is set, flip the crepe
  7. Spread hoisin sauce, soybean paste, and chili sauce on the crepe
  8. Add crispy wonton wrappers, coriander, and lettuce if using
  9. Fold the crepe into a roll or triangle
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Jian Bing!
